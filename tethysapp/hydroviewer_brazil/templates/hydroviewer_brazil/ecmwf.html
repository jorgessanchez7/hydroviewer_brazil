About HydroViewer

This web application was created to allow users to visualize hydrologic model results from different sources. It currently displays GloFAS and LDAS model results.

How it works

1. Click on Get Started.
2. Select a hydrologic model and a corresponding watershed of interest.
3. Once you click select a watershed, the map will automatically zoom to that specific watershed.
4. Click on any of the stream reaches within the watershed.
6. A hydrograph for the selected reach with the most current streamflow result will be displayed in the graph area next to the map.
7. If previous dates are available, the Select Date dropdown will be displayed on top of the graph. Select any of the available dates to display the forecast for that specific date in the graph.
8. Click the close button to exit the application.

About Streams

Streams refer to the watercourses or rivers that are displayed on the map. HydroViewer is a tool that allows you to visualize and analyze hydrological data, such as water levels, streamflows, and other variables related to rivers, streams, and watersheds.
When using HydroViewer, you can select a particular watershed or region and view the hydrological data associated with it. The streams are the main elements on the map, representing the watercourses where the hydrological data is being displayed.
The streams on HydroViewer provide a spatial representation of the watercourses and allow you to observe and analyze the hydrological data along these waterways. You can explore the data along specific streams, identify patterns, assess changes over time, and gain insights into the hydrological characteristics of the area.
By visualizing the streams and their associated hydrological data on HydroViewer, you can better understand the flow dynamics, water levels, and other relevant information for water resources management, flood forecasting, and decision-making processes related to rivers and streams.
